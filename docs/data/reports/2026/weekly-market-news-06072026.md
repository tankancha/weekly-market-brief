# Weekly Market News — 06/07/2026

## Executive Summary

The week of June 1–7, 2026 was defined by the Iran war's structural grip on global energy markets — now entering its 100th day with ceasefire talks stalled — alongside a sharp late-week reversal in US equities driven by twin shocks: Broadcom's disappointing AI chip guidance and a blowout May jobs report. Five significant market-moving events were identified. The dominant regime is **stagflationary**: WTI oil at ~$91/bbl (+50% pre-war) is sustaining US CPI at 3.8% YoY (3-year high), while a labor market printing 172,000 jobs vs. an 80,000 consensus has pushed year-end Fed rate hike odds to 70% and pushed 10-year Treasury yields back above 4.5%. The S&P 500 reached a record above 7,600 on June 2 — extending nine consecutive weeks of AI-driven gains — before reversing hard on June 5: Nasdaq –4.18% (worst single day since April 2025), S&P 500 –2.64%.

## Detailed Event Analysis

### 1. Iran War / Strait of Hormuz Oil Supply Disruption (Impact Score: 45)

**Event Date:** Began ~Feb 28, 2026; 100-day milestone June 7, 2026
**Event Type:** Geopolitical / Commodity Supply Shock
**News Source:** IEA Official Statement (Tier 1), Reuters, Bloomberg, CNBC, IMF Blog, Allianz Research

#### Event Summary

The 2026 Iran war has produced what the IEA describes as "the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market." The Strait of Hormuz — through which ~20–25% of global oil and ~20% of global LNG flows — remains effectively closed. As of June 7 (100th day), ceasefire negotiations between the US and Iran have stalled: Iranian FM Araghchi reported "no meaningful progress," and Iran-backed Hezbollah rejected a US-mediated proposal between Israel and Lebanon. The disruption has driven US CPI to 3.8% YoY via a 17.9% energy component surge (3-year high), raising stagflation risk across advanced economies. Chinese crude imports fell to their lowest level in ten years.

#### Market Reaction

**Immediate & Sustained:**

- **Oil:** WTI crude ~$91/bbl (+50% pre-war from ~$60); Brent crude ~$101/bbl (+36% from ~$74); EIA projects Brent averaging ~$106/b through June
- **Equities:** Initial global selloff at war onset; partially reversed during April–May AI earnings rally (S&P 500 +10.4% in April); S&P 500 broke record 7,600+ by June 2 despite oil headwinds
- **Gold:** Surged to $4,700+ on initial safe-haven bid; now at $4,366 (–6.79% over 4 weeks) as rising real yields and USD strength outweigh geopolitical premium
- **Bonds:** 10Y Treasury elevated at 4.5%+ partly inflation-driven; QT continuing
- **Currencies:** DXY ~99.4, supported by safe-haven demand and inflation-fueled rate hold expectations
- **Volatility:** VIX elevated during initial shock; partially normalized during AI rally

**Follow-Through:** Structural — no resolution signal within 30 days; oil price floor remains elevated.

**Pattern Comparison:** Amplified vs. historical precedent. IMF explicitly invokes the 1970s energy crisis framing. US net oil exporter status partially buffers domestic supply; impact heaviest on Asia and Europe. Strait of Hormuz closure at this scale is unprecedented.

#### Impact Assessment Detail

**Price Impact:** Severe — WTI +50%, Brent +36%, CPI at 3.8%
**Breadth:** Systemic — every major asset class affected across all geographies
**Forward Significance:** Regime Change — stagflation environment forcing global central banks to maintain/tighten policy despite economic slowdown risk
**Calculated Score:** (10 × 3) × 1.50 = **45**

#### Sector-Specific Impacts

- **Energy:** E&P majors, LNG shippers — dramatic outperformers
- **Airlines / Transport:** Severe negative from jet fuel cost surge
- **Consumer Discretionary:** Margin compression + demand headwinds from gasoline costs
- **Semiconductors / Tech:** Indirect negative — inflation suppresses risk appetite and rate cut optionality

#### Commodity Correlation

Oil: ~$91 WTI / ~$101 Brent; direct mechanism is Hormuz closure. Historical precedent: 1973 Arab Oil Embargo (S&P –40%+; DXY surged). Key distinction: US net oil exporter status buffers domestic supply. China impact: crude imports at 10-year lows reflecting refinery slowdown. Duration: Ceasefire talks collapsed — no resolution within 30 days.

**Event Type:** Economic Data (Employment)
**News Source:** Bureau of Labor Statistics — BLS.gov (Tier 1), CNBC, Reuters, FXStreet

- **Currencies:** USD surged; EUR/USD largest 1-day decline in over a week; GBP/USD fell below 1.34; DXY near 99.4
- **Volatility:** VIX spiked

**Follow-Through:** Sustained — CME FedWatch year-end rate hike probability jumped to 70%; bond yields held elevated into weekly close.

**Pattern Comparison:** Consistent — hot labor data producing "good news is bad news" dynamic for equities. Amplified by simultaneous Broadcom semiconductor shock on June 5.

#### Impact Assessment Detail

**Price Impact:** Major — 10Y +6bps, 2Y +11bps, USD +0.5%+, gold –2%
**Breadth:** Cross-Asset — bonds, equities, currencies, commodities moved simultaneously
**Forward Significance:** Trend Confirmation — reinforces "higher for longer"; 70% year-end hike probability

**Pattern Comparison:** Consistent with prior AI-guidance miss patterns (e.g., semis guidance misses in 2023–2024). "Good current results, bad forward guidance" reaction is well-established in high-expectation sectors. Amplified by concurrent NFP shock on June 5.

## Thematic Synthesis

### Dominant Market Narrative

The dominant theme of June 1–7, 2026 is **stagflationary pressure meeting AI euphoria — and winning, at least for the week.** The S&P 500 powered through nine consecutive weeks of gains to record highs above 7,600, carried by an extraordinary Q1 2026 earnings season (28.6% blended EPS growth — highest in nearly five years, led by Google Cloud +63% YoY to $20B), while markets largely looked past the Iran war oil shock. The June 5 twin-shock — Broadcom's AI chip revenue ceiling and a 115% NFP beat that crushed rate-cut hopes — broke the streak decisively. The Nasdaq's –4.18% single-day loss signals that even the AI mega-narrative has price limits.

### Interconnected Events

All five ranked events converge on one outcome: **higher for longer, everywhere:**

- Iran war → oil +50% → US CPI 3.8% → Fed cannot cut; ECB must hike
- NFP 172K vs 80K → labor resilience → 70% year-end hike probability
- Broadcom AI guidance ceiling → AI revenue deceleration concern → risk-off in tech
- ECB 99% hike priced → global synchronized tightening cycle
- Warsh at Fed → potential for more aggressive tightening reaction function

The oil shock (Rank 1) is the root cause cascading into all others. Without it, CPI would likely be at or below 2.5%, giving the Fed room to cut — which would significantly support tech/growth valuations and reverse the current narrative.

### Market Regime Assessment

**Risk Appetite:** Mixed — Risk-On via AI earnings through May, then decisive Risk-Off reversal on June 5
**Evidence:**

- Positive: S&P 500 all-time high 7,600 on June 2; Q1 EPS growth 28.6%; nine-week consecutive rally
- Negative: Nasdaq –4.7% for week; gold falling on real rate rise (not classic geopolitical safe-haven behavior); DXY near 99.4

**Sector Rotation:**

- **Leaders:** Energy, Financials (rate beneficiaries), Healthcare (defensive)
- **Laggards:** Semiconductors, long-duration tech, Real Estate/REITs
- **Growth vs Value:** Rotation accelerating toward value on June 5

### Commodity Deep Dive

- **Energy:** WTI ~$91/bbl, Brent ~$101/bbl. Iran war/Hormuz closure is the singular driver. EIA projects Brent averaging ~$106/b through May-June. Chinese crude imports at 10-year lows (refinery slowdown + reduced Hormuz flows). No ceasefire signal within 30 days. OPEC+ largely bypassed as the supply disruption is geopolitical, not OPEC-driven.
- **Precious Metals:** Gold at $4,366/oz, down 6.79% over 4 weeks and –4% for the week. Anomaly: gold is declining during an active geopolitical crisis, overridden by rising real yields (10Y nominal 4.5%+) and a strong DXY (~99.4). The real rate channel is dominating the safe-haven channel.
- **Base Metals:** Copper under pressure — China's 10-year low crude imports reflect broader industrial demand slowdown; bearish signal for global manufacturing activity.
- **Agricultural:** Not a primary market driver this week.

### Anomalies and Surprises

1. **Gold declining during an active war:** Gold –4% for the week and –6.79% over 4 weeks despite the largest oil supply disruption in history. Driven by rising real yields and USD strength outweighing safe-haven demand. Watch: if ceasefire appears, oil crashes → CPI drops → rate cuts priced → gold could surge 5–10%.
2. **S&P 500 hitting all-time highs at CPI 3.8%:** Nine-week rally to 7,600 occurred despite 3.8% inflation and Fed on hold. AI earnings momentum (28.6% blended EPS growth) dominated macro headwinds — until Broadcom tested the limits of that narrative.
3. **NFP beat of +115% over consensus:** 172K vs. 80K is a historically rare miss magnitude — models significantly underestimated labor market resilience in an oil shock environment, likely because US net energy exporter status shields domestic labor demand from the full import-price shock.
