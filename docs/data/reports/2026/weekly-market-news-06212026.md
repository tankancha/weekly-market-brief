## Detailed Event Analysis

### 1. US-Iran Peace Deal / Strait of Hormuz Reopening (Impact Score: 31.5)

**Event Date:** June 18–21, 2026
**Event Type:** Geopolitical — Conflict Resolution
**News Source:** Al Jazeera, Reuters, NPR, JP Morgan Research (Tier 1)

#### Event Summary

Pakistan announced on June 18 that a US-Iran MOU to end the war includes Tehran immediately reopening the Strait of Hormuz and a halt to the US blockade of Iranian ports. President Trump, Iranian leaders, and Pakistani mediators all confirmed a formal signing planned for Friday June 20 in Switzerland. Switzerland talks were subsequently canceled, introducing renewed uncertainty and a brief Brent bounce to ~$80. Despite the diplomatic wobble, tankers carrying previously stranded Iranian crude began exiting the Strait, providing real-world confirmation of the ceasefire. The IEA had characterized the Hormuz closure as "the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market," disrupting ~20% of global oil supply and significant LNG volumes. Brent peaked at ~$120/bbl during the conflict; it now trades near $80, with WTI at ~$77 as of June 19.

#### Market Reaction

**Immediate (Week-of):**

- **Energy:** WTI ~$77 (Jun 19), Brent ~$80 — down ~33% from $120 wartime peak; sharpest weekly crude decline of 2026
- **Equities:** Risk-on rotation; S&P 500 +0.9% for week to 7,500.58; energy sector re-rated lower; broader market rallied on inflation relief
- **Bonds:** 10Y Treasury yield eased marginally as oil-driven inflation premium compressed
- **Gold:** $4,155 (Jun 21) vs $4,460 (Jun 1) — down 6.8% month-to-date. Paradoxically bearish: Iran war had supercharged inflation expectations → pricing out Fed cuts → stronger USD → gold headwinds; peace deal removes residual safe-haven premium
- **FX:** DXY ~100.8 (1-year highs); Hormuz resolution had limited additional USD impact as hawkish Fed dominated
- **Volatility:** VIX declined as geopolitical tail-risk premium compressed

**Follow-Through:** Incomplete resolution — Switzerland talks canceled adds fragility. Tankers exiting Hormuz is the strongest confirmation; formal signing remains pending.

**Pattern Comparison:** **Consistent** with historical geopolitical resolution playbook — energy prices reverting toward pre-conflict levels, equities rallying, safe havens softening.

#### Impact Assessment Detail

**Price Impact:** Severe — Brent -33% from $120 peak to ~$80
**Breadth:** Systemic (3×) — energy, inflation, G3 central bank policy, EM economies, bonds, equities, FX
**Forward Significance:** Regime Change (+50%) — end of the supply shock that drove synchronized global tightening
**Calculated Score:** (10 × 3) × 1.50 = **31.5**

#### Commodity Correlation

~20% of global oil supply and LNG disrupted via Hormuz. Peace deal → tankers resuming → Goldman Sachs Brent target $80 Q4 2026. Energy E&P earnings at risk as margins compress on lower oil. LNG prices easing — significant for European energy security. Gold fell counterintuitively as Iran war had driven real rates higher and USD stronger, overriding safe-haven bid.

### 2. ECB +25bps Rate Hike — First in Three Years (Impact Score: 21.0)

**Event Date:** June 11, 2026
**Event Type:** Monetary Policy
**News Source:** ECB.europa.eu, Euronews, Capital Economics (Tier 1 / Official)

#### Event Summary

The ECB Governing Council raised all three key rates by 25bps (deposit facility: 2.00%→2.25%; MRO: 2.15%→2.40%; marginal lending: 2.40%→2.65%), effective June 17. This reversed eight consecutive cuts between June 2024 and June 2025 — the sharpest ECB policy reversal since the 2011 mistake. Trigger: Iran War energy shock pushed euro area CPI to 3.2% in May 2026. New ECB baseline: inflation averaging 3.0% in 2026, 2.3% in 2027, 2.0% in 2028; GDP growth 0.8% in 2026 — stagflationary configuration. The decision was described as "robust across a range of scenarios" of how the conflict might evolve.

#### Market Reaction

**Immediate (Day-of):**

- **Bonds:** EZ sovereign yields surged +25bps; German Bund 10Y climbed; peripheral spreads (Italy, Spain) widened
- **EUR/USD:** Euro briefly firmed on rate differential, but stagflation growth outlook capped gains
- **Equities:** Euro Stoxx pressured; rate-sensitive sectors (real estate, utilities) led declines
- **Global:** Spilled into EM bond markets via risk repricing

**Follow-Through:** Hawkish signal amplified subsequent ECB commentary; markets pricing further potential hikes if energy shock persists.

**Pattern Comparison:** **Amplified** vs. historical first-hike reactions — consensus had expected continued cuts, making the surprise factor unusually high.

#### Impact Assessment Detail

**Price Impact:** Major — EZ bond yields +25bps intraday; equities sold off
**Breadth:** Cross-Asset (2×) — EUR rates, EUR/USD, EU equities, EM contagion
**Forward Significance:** Regime Change (+50%) — three-year easing cycle reversed; ECB joins global "higher for longer" consensus
**Calculated Score:** (7 × 2) × 1.50 = **21.0**

### 3. FOMC Hawkish Dot Plot Flip — Warsh Era Begins (Impact Score: 21.0)

**Event Date:** June 17, 2026
**Event Type:** Monetary Policy
**News Source:** FederalReserve.gov, CNBC, Advisor Perspectives (Tier 1 / Official)

#### Event Summary

The FOMC held rates unanimously at 3.50–3.75% in Chairman Kevin Warsh's inaugural meeting. The headline was not the hold but the dot plot revision: the median 2026 year-end projection rose to 3.8% from 3.4% in March — shifting from implying a cut to implying a hike relative to the current 3.625% midpoint. Nine of 18 officials now project at least one 2026 rate increase. The statement cited solid economic activity, productivity and capital investment strength, steady job gains, and inflation "remaining elevated relative to the 2% goal, in part reflecting supply shocks from the Middle East." Traders repriced a hike as early as October 2026. This marks a fundamental pivot: the first FOMC dot plot to indicate a potential hike since the 2022–2023 tightening cycle.

- **FX:** DXY climbed to ~100.8 — near 1-year highs; rate differential vs ECB/BOJ (who also hiked) compressed dollar gains
- **Commodities:** Gold pressured by stronger USD and higher real rate outlook

**Follow-Through:** Nasdaq led recovery via SpaceX/chip catalyst — "good macro is still good for AI" narrative dominated over rate fear.

**Pattern Comparison:** **Dampened** negative reaction — brief sell-the-news followed by buy; chip/AI enthusiasm offset rate headwinds.

#### Impact Assessment Detail

**Price Impact:** Major — dot plot regime change; 10Y yields moved; DXY at 1-year highs

### 4. Bank of Japan Hikes to 1.0% — Highest Since 1995 (Impact Score: 21.0)

**Event Date:** June 16, 2026
**Event Type:** Monetary Policy
**News Source:** BOJ.or.jp, CNBC, Bloomberg (Tier 1 / Official)

#### Event Summary

The BOJ raised its policy rate 25bps to 1.00% in a 7-1 vote (sole dissenter: Asada Toichiro, citing downside risks to production). The 1.0% level is the highest since September 1995 and marks Japan's most aggressive tightening stance in three decades. Japan's core CPI has exceeded 2% for 44 consecutive months; BOJ's FY2026 core CPI forecast: 2.5–3.0%, reflecting Iran War energy pass-through costs and sustained domestic wage growth. The BOJ stated it will continue raising rates as economic and price data warrant, closely monitoring the Middle East conflict. Bloomberg had reported in early June that BOJ was mulling a June hike with another possible later in 2026 — the decision came in line with those trial balloons.

#### Market Reaction

**Immediate (Day-of):**

- **JPY:** Yen strengthened materially — elevated carry cost deflates yen-funded carry trades globally
- **JGB:** 10Y Japanese Government Bond yield climbed to multi-decade highs
- **EM FX:** Yen carry unwind pressured EM currencies (unwinding of cheap JPY-funded risk positions)
- **Nikkei:** Japanese equities pressured short-term by stronger yen compressing export earnings
- **Global Rates:** Reinforced worldwide "higher for longer" narrative alongside ECB hike five days earlier

**Follow-Through:** JPY carry unwind is structural and ongoing — each incremental BOJ hike reduces the cost advantage of borrowing in JPY.

**Pattern Comparison:** **Consistent** with BOJ normalization playbook, though 1.0% exceeded consensus expectation of 0.75%.

#### Impact Assessment Detail

**Price Impact:** Major — JPY strengthened ~1–2%; JGB yields at 30-year highs
**Breadth:** Cross-Asset (2×) — JGB yields, JPY, EM FX, carry trades, Nikkei
**Forward Significance:** Regime Change (+50%) — BOJ exiting ZIRP/NIRP era after 30 years; JPY carry trade structural unwind accelerating

## Thematic Synthesis

### Dominant Market Narrative

The week's overarching theme: **geopolitical resolution colliding with synchronized global monetary tightening.** The Iran War supply shock that drove oil to $120, forced three major central banks to hike simultaneously, and created the worst energy supply disruption in recorded history is beginning to unwind. Markets are repricing two simultaneous signals: (1) energy prices falling → inflation relief → equity tailwind; (2) rates globally moving higher → cost of capital rising → multiple compression headwind. The net result is a cautious risk-on with significant dispersion: Nasdaq technology outperforming, energy sector capped, rate-sensitive sectors under pressure.

### Interconnected Events

The Iran War is the causal root linking four of five ranked events:

- **Iran War → energy shock → ECB hike** (first in 3 years to combat 3.2% CPI)
- **Iran War → energy shock → BOJ hike** (energy pass-through into 44 months of above-2% CPI)
- **Iran War → energy shock → FOMC dot plot flip** (inflation staying elevated, pricing out 2026 cut)
- **Iran War peace deal → oil -33% from peak → inflation relief → equity rally**
- **SpaceX IPO** is an independent catalyst reinforcing risk appetite and Nasdaq leadership

**Risk Appetite:** Mixed → Transitioning to Cautious Risk-On
**Evidence:** S&P 500 at 7,500 (11 of 12 weeks positive); Nasdaq +2.4%; VIX declining; but DXY at 1-year highs (~100.8), gold at $4,155 (6.8% MTD decline) — not a full risk-on rotation
**Sector Rotation:** Tech / semiconductors outperforming; energy sector capped by oil price decline; rate-sensitive sectors (REITs, utilities) under pressure from triple central bank hawkish turn; financials benefiting from higher-rate environment
