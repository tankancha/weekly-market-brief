# Weekly Market News — 06/28/2026

## Executive Summary

Over June 22–28, 2026, the dominant theme was the **violent unwind of the 2026 energy shock**: with the Strait of Hormuz reopening and a US Treasury 60-day license restoring Iranian crude flows, Brent fell more than 10% on the week and WTI broke to **$68.86 — its lowest since February** (−22% over four weeks). The disinflation relief pulled the 10-year Treasury yield down ~7bp even as **May PCE printed a hot 4.1% YoY** (the highest since April 2023) on lagging energy pass-through. Equities were bifurcated: the Dow held up on cheaper oil while the **Nasdaq fell for a 5th straight session** on a mega-cap tech rotation, partially cushioned by **Micron's blowout (+15%)**. The Jun 16–17 BOJ hike to 1.0% and the Warsh-led FOMC's hawkish dot-plot remain the policy backdrop framing the week. Of nine candidate events, five cleared the materiality threshold; the oil crash was decisively the highest-impact.

## Detailed Event Analysis

### 1. Oil Crash — WTI Breaks $69 as Hormuz Reopens (Impact Score: 37.5)

**Event Date:** June 22–26, 2026
**Event Type:** Geopolitical / Commodity
**News Source:** Al Jazeera, Fortune, TradingEconomics (Tier 1–2)

#### Event Summary
The US–Iran de-escalation moved from agreement to physical supply normalization. A US Treasury 60-day license authorized Iranian oil production, sale, and US imports; Persian Gulf exports recovered to ~75% of pre-war levels and Saudi Arabia resumed loading at Ras Tanura. With ~1/5 of global seaborne oil transiting Hormuz now flowing again, the "$200 oil" tail risk that had dominated H1 2026 collapsed.

#### Market Reaction

**Immediate (Day-of):**
- **Commodities:** Brent ~$79.25 (Jun 22) → ~$72 (Fri), >10% weekly drop, the largest in a month; WTI ~$78.94 → **$68.86** (lowest since February). Wed Jun 24 alone: Brent −4.33%, WTI −3.92%.
- **Bonds:** 10Y yield fell ~7bp to ~4.37% as the energy-inflation premium drained.
- **Equities:** Dow resilient (Caterpillar +~4% Mon) on lower input costs; energy stocks pressured.
- **Gold:** Eased toward ~$4,087 as the war/safe-haven premium unwound (−8.3% MTD).
- **Volatility:** VIX subdued (~16–17).

**Follow-Through:** Sustained — the move accelerated into Friday with WTI at a multi-month low.

**Pattern Comparison:** Consistent and **Amplified** versus the classic supply-restoration template. The speed reflected positioning: funds had carried a large war-risk premium that unwound rapidly once physical barrels confirmed the narrative.

#### Impact Assessment Detail
**Price Impact:** Severe — a >10% weekly Brent move and a sub-$69 WTI print far exceed the ±5% severe threshold.
**Breadth:** Systemic — energy, rates, equities, gold, and FX all repriced.
**Forward Significance:** Trend Confirmation — completes the disinflationary normalization of the 2026 energy shock.
**Calculated Score:** (10 × 3) × 1.25 = 37.5

#### Sector-Specific Impacts
Energy producers and oilfield services fell; transports, airlines, and consumer discretionary benefited from lower fuel costs. The cheaper-oil tailwind is disinflationary for headline CPI into Q3.

#### Commodity Correlation
Restored Hormuz throughput is the textbook supply-side driver: an estimated multi-million-bpd return of barrels against still-soft demand. Saudi spare-capacity reactivation adds a structural lid. Expect WTI to base in the high-$60s/low-$70s absent re-escalation; the prior $200 fear scenario is off the table for now.

### 2. May PCE +4.1% YoY — Hottest Since April 2023 (Impact Score: 17.5)

**Event Date:** June 25, 2026
**Event Type:** Economic Data (Inflation)
**News Source:** CNBC, CBS News, BEA (Tier 1)

#### Event Summary
Headline PCE rose +0.4% m/m and **+4.1% YoY** — the hottest since April 2023 — with Core PCE at +3.4% (above the 3.3% estimate) and personal income +0.7% (vs +0.4% est). Iran-driven oil and gasoline were cited as reigniting the Fed's preferred gauge, validating the FOMC's hawkish tilt.

#### Market Reaction

**Immediate (Day-of):**
- **Bonds:** 10Y yield fell ~7bp on the day; the print read roughly in-line, so traders modestly scaled back the probability of an October hike.
- **USD:** DXY held near a >1-year high (~101.5).
- **Gold:** Eased alongside the lower war premium.
- **Equities:** Muted — the in-line interpretation dominated the headline shock.

**Follow-Through:** Consolidated; the oil collapse simultaneously argued for cooler future prints, offsetting the hot back-data.

**Pattern Comparison:** **Dampened** versus the typical hot-PCE selloff — the market looked through a backward-looking, energy-driven number toward disinflation already visible in spot crude.

#### Impact Assessment Detail
**Price Impact:** Major — the Fed's preferred gauge at a 3-year high, though reaction was contained.
**Breadth:** Cross-Asset — bonds, USD, gold, equities.
**Forward Significance:** Trend Confirmation — sticky inflation keeps the Fed's no-cut/possible-hike stance intact.
**Calculated Score:** (7 × 2) × 1.25 = 17.5

#### Sector-Specific Impacts
Rate-sensitive growth equities stayed on the back foot; the lack of a dovish pivot kept duration-heavy sectors capped even as yields ticked lower on the oil offset.

## Thematic Synthesis

### Dominant Market Narrative
**Disinflation relief through energy.** The week's defining force was the physical normalization of oil supply via a reopened Strait of Hormuz, which crushed crude (WTI sub-$69) and drained the war-risk premium across assets. Lower oil eased the inflation impulse even as backward-looking May PCE ran hot at 4.1% — the market correctly looked through stale data toward cooler forward prints.

### Interconnected Events
The events were **reinforcing**: cheaper oil (#1) reframed the hot PCE (#2) as a peaking, not accelerating, signal and pulled yields lower; lower yields plus a firm GDP (#5) supported cyclicals/Dow over mega-cap tech, feeding the Nasdaq rotation (#4); Micron's blowout (#3) was the lone idiosyncratic offset within an otherwise out-of-favor growth complex.

### Market Regime Assessment
**Risk Appetite:** Mixed — disinflation-relief tape with internal rotation.
**Evidence:** Dow resilient and 10Y lower (risk-supportive), but Nasdaq down five straight and DXY at a >1-year high (cautious); VIX calm.
**Sector Rotation:** Out of mega-cap tech/AI momentum, into defensives, industrials, transports, and oil-relief cyclicals. Memory (Micron) bucked the trend.

### Commodity Deep Dive
- **Energy:** Brent −10%+/wk to ~$72; WTI to $68.86 (−22% over four weeks). Driver: Hormuz reopening + Saudi ramp. A structural lid now sits over crude absent re-escalation.
- **Precious Metals:** Gold ~$4,087 (−8.3% MTD, ~25% below the Jan ATH of $5,589) as the safe-haven premium unwound.
- **Base Metals / Agri:** Quiet week; no fresh catalyst.

### Anomalies and Surprises
The **dampened reaction to a 3-year-high PCE** was the notable surprise — markets prioritized the live oil signal over backward-looking inflation. Micron overtaking Meta/Tesla by market cap intraday underscored how concentrated AI-memory enthusiasm has become.
